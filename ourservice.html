Our service

We offer a variety of regulated care and support services that aim to meet the holistic needs of those who have mental health needs and/or who are learning disabled.

We also support those who have additional needs such as dual diagnosis, physical disabilities, autistic spectrum disorders and sensory impairments. In addition, we specialise in providing support to people who may be regarded as having “challenging behaviour”.

The services we offer are flexible and range from intensive support packages for people requiring round the clock support, to specific task focused support for a few hours at a time. 

Our models of support


You can receive our service in a residential home, through supported-living accommodations or in your current home. Here's all the various options available.

Residential Care

A safe and caring home environment with 24- hour round the clock support, our registered care home, Clover House provides 6 long-term placements for those with mental health needs and/or learning disabilities.

Supported Living

We work with our service users in single occupancy and shared tenancies in partnership with various social landlords across the north of Lancashire to provide 24-hour staff and a flexible level of one-to-one support.

Homecare

This is a flexible approach to domiciliary care where we work with you in your own home, your workplace and/or the community to help mantain your independence during the times and days when you need us.

Rehabilitation

A 24-month programme to help clients to develop and regain their social, emotional and physical wellbeing. Led by a mental health nurse, our rehabilitation team use the Recovery Star framework to achieve independence.

Overview of some of the services we offer

Developing Independent Living Skills

  • Developing and achieving goal focused learning programmes
  • Teaching independent living skills in a user focused way
  • Building and developing skills for independent living

Domestic Support

  • Support people with cleaning their homes and do laundry
  • Assist and support with grocery shopping and meal preparation and cooking skills

Emotional and Behavioural Support  

  • Developing and assisting with behavioural support programmes
  • Assisting people to develop their social skills 
  • Helping to maintain positive relationships with family, friends and in the community
  • Supporting and encouraging service users to take part in recreational activities, inside and outside their home

Educational and Employment Support

  • Supporting service users to access and attend college courses
  • Assisting people to find and attend employment
  • Supporting service users with work placements and vocational training opportunities

Community and Leisure Support

  • Assisting people access community facilities such as libraries, gyms and clubs
  • Supporting people to develop social skills and to build up community connections and friendships
  • Helping people to gain confidence and increase their self-esteem in the community

Managing Health and Wellbeing

  • Support people to arrange and attend medical appointments, and to implement advice, guidance and follow up action. 
  • Assist service users with their personal care
  • Help service users to live healthy, safe lives, and to recognise and manage risks as they arise
  • Support service users to manage their own medication, and to administer this where appropriate.  

Support with Housing and Community

  • Support people to maintain positive relationships with neighbours and co-tenants
  • Help people to keep their homes safe and free from hazards
  • Assist people to find suitable accommodation to meet their needs and work with housing associations / landlords to support people to manage their tenancy

Support to Manage Money

  • Support to claim benefits and other entitlements
  • Help to open, access and manage bank accounts
  • Assist with budgeting and paying bills

Emergency Placements and On-call Support

  • 24 hour on-call support with an Immediate response service
  • Out of hours emergency placements / support
  • Emergency staff cover at short notice
  • Working with assistive technology to provide economical, discrete support and peace of mind
